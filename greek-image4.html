Ganymede jewelry

Title: Ganymede jewelry Period: Hellenistic Date: ca. 330–300 BCE Culture: Greek Medium: Gold, rock crystal, emerald Dimensions: length of necklace 13 in. (33 cm); H. of earrings 2 3/8 in. (6 cm); width of bracelets 3 1/8 in. (8 cm); width of fibulae 1 15/16 in. (5 cm); H. of ring 13/16 in. (2.1 cm) Classification: Gold and Silver Credit Line: Harris Brisbane Dick Fund, 1937 Accession Number: 37.11.8–.17

The Ganymede Jewelry collection, part of the Metropolitan Museum of Art's holdings, showcases exquisite craftsmanship inspired by ancient Greek mythology and artistry. Named after Ganymede, the beautiful youth abducted by Zeus to serve as cupbearer to the gods, these pieces often incorporate motifs of celestial and divine themes. Crafted with meticulous detail, the jewelry exemplifies the Hellenistic flair for combining luxurious materials like gold, gemstones, and enameling with symbolic designs. These adornments not only reflect the cultural and artistic sophistication of their time but also echo a timeless allure, making them a fascinating fusion of mythology and craftsmanship.
